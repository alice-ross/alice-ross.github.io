About

I moved to Edinburgh in 2002 - yes, 2002, not a typo - to study for my undergraduate degree, MA (Hons) Linguistics at the University of Edinburgh. I loved both the subject and the city, so after graduating, I stayed here and tried employment as a way to support my habit of paying the rent. I worked in various not very interesting jobs in book publishing, taking photos of tartan, taking photos of cheese, and the voluntary sector, then accepted a six-month temporary QA contract at AAA video game studio Rockstar North, ended up as a senior producer and stayed there for almost ten years.

By 2021 I had decided it was time for a career change and I've had the incredible good fortune of being able to return to Edinburgh's world-class Linguistics department again, this time for a one-year MSc in Developmental Linguistics; it's been fantastic to study with some of the people who inspired me the first time around as well as a whole new set of wonderful teachers and colleagues. The world has changed, as have I, in the last two decades, and I'm particularly interested in the rise of natural language interfaces as a completely new speech context and a new way of relating to machines.

When I'm not doing science you might find me appreciating cats, reading, in the sea, modelling for artists, adding to my extensive tattoo collection, forest bathing, or studying Yiddish.
